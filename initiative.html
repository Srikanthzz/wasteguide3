India has launched several initiatives to manage waste effectively:

Government Initiatives

1. Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (2014): A national cleanliness campaign aiming to make India open-defecation free and promote waste management.
2. Solid Waste Management Rules (2016): Regulates waste segregation, collection, and disposal.
3. Plastic Waste Management Rules (2016): Bans plastic bags, straws, and cutlery.
4. National Clean Air Programme (2019): Targets reducing air pollution through waste management.
5. Waste-to-Wealth Mission: Converts waste into energy, biofuels, and other valuable products.

City-specific Initiatives

1. Mumbai's Waste-to-Energy Plant: Converts 600 tons of waste into electricity daily.
2. Delhi's Zero Waste Project: Aims to make Delhi waste-free by 2030.
3. Bangalore's Waste Segregation: Mandatory segregation of waste into three categories.
4. Chennai's Waste-to-Compost Initiative: Converts organic waste into compost.
5. Hyderabad's Waste-to-Energy Plant: Generates electricity from waste.

Community-driven Initiatives

1. Kerala's Zero Waste Villages: Villages adopting waste-free practices.
2. Tamil Nadu's Self-Help Groups: Women-led groups managing waste collection and composting.
3. Gujarat's Waste Warriors: Community-driven waste collection and segregation.
4. Pune's SWaCH Cooperative: Waste pickers' cooperative managing waste collection.

Innovative Solutions

1. Waste-to-Biofuel Plants: Converting waste into biofuels.
2. Smart Bins: Solar-powered waste collection bins.
3. Waste-to-Electricity Plants: Generating electricity from waste.
4. Biodegradable Waste Management: Using microorganisms to decompose organic waste.
5. Waste Recycling Apps: Connecting waste generators with recyclers.

Key Organizations

1. Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB)
2. Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC)
3. National Environmental Policy (NEP)
4. Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) research initiatives
5. Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) like Waste Warriors, SWaCH, and others.
